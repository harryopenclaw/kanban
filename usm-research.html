What Is a USM?

The Unique Speaking Mechanism (USM) is a concept popularized by Taylor Conroy, a 4x TEDx speaker and co-founder of Leadr.co (formerly Thought-Leader). It's the core framework he uses to help 700+ clients land TEDx talks and build thought leadership platforms.

Your USM is not your topic. It's the deeper driving force beneath your topic — the thing that makes you the person who must deliver this message. It's the intersection of your lived experience, your passion, and the unique lens through which you see the world.

"There's the topic that you speak about, the thing that makes sense for your business. But underneath that topic, there's your Unique Speaking Mechanism. This is the thing that you would die for. This is the thing that actually fuels everything in your life."
— Taylor Conroy

Topic vs. USM — The Key Distinction

❌ Topic (Surface Level)

  • • "I speak about leadership"
  • • "I talk about marketing"
  • • "My topic is wellness"
  • • Generic, replaceable — anyone could claim it
  • • Doesn't explain why you

✅ USM (Deep Level)

  • • The personal mission that fuels the topic
  • • Rooted in your life story and experience
  • • The thing that makes you irreplaceable on stage
  • • What you'd champion even if no one paid you
  • • Explains why audiences should listen to you specifically

Example: A business coach's topic might be "scaling startups." Their USM might be rooted in watching their parents lose everything in a failed business — driving a mission to ensure no family goes through that again. The USM is what makes the talk unforgettable.

How to Discover Your USM

Based on Conroy's framework and public teachings:

1

Identify Your Topic

What do you naturally speak about? What does your business or expertise center on? This is your surface-level starting point.

2

Ask "Why Does This Matter to Me?"

Go beneath the topic. What personal experience, trauma, breakthrough, or conviction drives you to care about this more than the average person? This is usually a story from your past.

3

Find the "Would Die For" Test

Conroy says your USM is "the thing you would die for." If you stripped away the business, the money, the audience — what message would you still need the world to hear?

4

Connect USM → Talk → Brand

Your USM becomes the emotional backbone of your signature talk, your content, and your personal brand. Everything flows from it — it's not a marketing angle, it's your identity as a speaker.
