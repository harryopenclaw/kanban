2. JPMorgan Chase Institute: Small Biz AI Spending Data Research

Published Apr 14, 2026. Using 4.6M firm transaction data: the 2025 cohort reached 10% AI adoption in just 6 months (vs 77 months for 2019 cohort). 17.7% of all small businesses now pay for AI services. GenAI dominates spending across every industry. Knowledge-intensive sectors (info, professional services) lead at 30-39% adoption.

3. Federal Reserve: 18% Firm Adoption, 41% Workforce Using GenAI Research

Published Apr 3, 2026. Three-survey analysis (Census BTOS, Real-Time Population Survey, SBU): 18% of firms adopted AI; 41% of workers use GenAI at work; 78% of the labor force works at firms that have adopted AI. Professional services and finance lead. Adoption is accelerating faster than electricity, PCs, or the internet.

federalreserve.gov
4. ChatGPT Ads Launch — New Channel for Local Business Advertising

OpenAI began testing ads in ChatGPT (Jan 2026) for Free and Go tier users. Contextual (not keyword) ad model with "Answer Independence" — AI answers aren't influenced by ad spend. Early-mover local businesses can reach high-intent users in conversational context at low CPCs before the platform matures.

5. Main Street America: 8 Trends for 2026 Trends

K-shaped economy pressuring small biz; social media fatigue driving experiential retail; e-commerce brands moving to brick-and-mortar; circular retail expanding beyond apparel; younger generations gaining spending power. AI adoption among Main Street programs is higher than among the businesses they serve — a content opportunity gap.

mainstreet.org
6. The Adoption-Gap Content Play Insight

82% of micro-businesses (<5 employees) believe AI "isn't applicable" to them — an education gap, not a real limitation. Only 14% of SMBs have fully integrated AI into core ops despite 76% using it. The massive middle ground of "curious but stuck" businesses is the ideal content audience for Immediac.

💡 Content Ideas (Generated from Trends)
A. "The AI for Main Street Act — What It Actually Means for Your Business" Explainer

Break down the legislation in plain language. Cover the 4 programs (loans, tax credit, training vouchers, resource centers). CTA: book a free SBDC advisory session. Format: short-form video (60s) + LinkedIn carousel + blog post.

B. "82% of Small Businesses Think AI Isn't for Them. Here's Why They're Wrong." Hook

Use the Hormozi proof+promise hook: "We helped [X] local businesses save 20+ hours/month with AI. Here's exactly what they did." Show 3 simple, boring use cases (email drafting, review responses, ad copy). Format: Reel + LinkedIn text post.

C. "AI Adoption Is Faster Than Electricity, PCs, or the Internet — Here's the Data" Data Story

Visualize the JPMorgan/Fed data: 2025 cohort hit 10% adoption in 6 months vs 77 months for 2019. Use as authority-building content for Immediac's AI expertise. Format: carousel with charts + YouTube explainer.

D. "ChatGPT Now Shows Ads — What Local Businesses Need to Know" News Take

First-mover angle: CPCs are low now, conversational context > keyword matching, "Answer Independence" means trust is preserved. CTA: Immediac can help you test this channel. Format: short-form video + blog post.

E. "The 90-Day AI Playbook for Main Street Businesses" Lead Magnet

Days 1-30: advisory + self-assessment. Days 31-60: pilot one AI tool. Days 61-90: measure + expand. Offer as a downloadable PDF (email capture). Promote via all channels. Format: long-form blog + downloadable guide + email sequence.

F. "Social Media Is Losing Its Grip — What Replaces It for Local Business?" POV

Tie Main Street America's social media fatigue trend to Immediac's positioning: website, Google profile, and word-of-mouth are becoming more important again. AI helps you optimize those channels. Format: LinkedIn article + podcast segment.
